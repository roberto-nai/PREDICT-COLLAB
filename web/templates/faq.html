What types of predictions are available? +
  • Next activity that is likely to occur in the process: The prediction indicates the possible next activity.
  • Next participant that is likely to send a message: The prediction indicates only the participant who is likely to send the next message.
  • Next participant that is likely to send a message (with activity): The prediction indicates the activity and the participant corresponding to the possible next message.
  • Next activity that is likely to occur in the process and its participant: The prediction indicates the possible next activity and the participant.
  • Next participant that is likely to act: The prediction indicates only the participant who is likely to perform the next activity.
  • Time until the next event: The prediction indicates the time remaining until the next event occurs.
  • Time until the next message to send: The prediction indicates the time remaining until the next message is sent.
  • Process remaining time: The prediction indicates the time remaining until the activities of the case are completed.
  • Participant remaining time: The prediction indicates the time remaining until the participant's activities in the case are completed.
What format should the entered names follow?+
The names for the log file, process (Logs management), and model (Models management) must comply with the format expressed by the regular expression: ^[a-zA-Z0-9-_]+$
What do I need to upload a new log? +
Process name, description, and a file in CSV or XES format
Can I delete a log from the system?+
Yes, as long as it has no associated models
Is there any precondition for the log to generate a model?+
Yes, to generate the model correctly, the log must contain case identifiers (Case ID) and a timestamp (Time) associated with each event. The events in each trace must be ordered by their timestamp.
Can multiple logs be uploaded for the same process?+
Yes, as long as the associated files have different names
What do I need to create a model?+
A process must have been created and associated with an event log in the Logs management section.
Can I modify a model?+
No, it can be deleted and recreated, or a new model can be created with the desired characteristics.
What is the "Time format" field used for when creating a model?+
This field is used only for time predictions and is related to the unit in which the prediction result is expected. Keep in mind that in some processes, cases are completed in seconds or minutes, while others may take several days. Choose the option that best suits your reality.
What do I need to create a prediction?+
An event log associated with the process must be loaded, a model for the desired prediction must be generated, and a file of ongoing traces must be uploaded in the same format as the selected event log.
Are the predictions made stored in the system?+
No, but after making the prediction, it is possible to download the results.
